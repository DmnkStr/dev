WingSim is a highly immersive, full-body VR wingsuit flight simulator developed by SimVenture, combining physical motion systems with real-time visual simulation to recreate authentic flight experiences. The system uses a rope-driven SimSuit and advanced physical modeling to simulate all phases of a wingsuit flight, from jump to landing, while synchronizing sensory feedback for realism and minimal latency.

During my time at SomaReality, I was responsible for the real-time visualisation layer in Unreal Engine 5. I created a high-fidelity 3D environment of the Dolomites using Blender and marketplace assets, aligning with one of the simulator's core scenic locations. I implemented a data integration pipeline by developing an adapter between a Redis database and UE5, enabling live ingestion of flight data generated by SimVenture's simulator.

I implemented two flight modes:

1. A real-time mode driven by simulator data streamed via Redis.
2. A replay mode based on pre-recorded GPS flight data, which I transformed into Unreal Engine coordinate space for accurate trajectory reproduction.

My work enabled a synchronized digital twin of real wingsuit flights, later used in marketing content where rendered UE5 footage was seamlessly matched with real FPV recordings, demonstrating a high level of visual and motion fidelity.
