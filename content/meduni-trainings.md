At Soma Reality, I worked as a 3D Generalist on VR-based medical training applications developed in collaboration with the Medical University of Vienna and industry partners.

My primary responsibility was the end-to-end creation of 3D content, including modeling, texturing, materials, shaders, environments, and characters. I ensured all assets met the visual and technical requirements for real-time performance on standalone VR hardware (Oculus Quest 2), balancing fidelity with strict optimization constraints.

I contributed to a VR simulation for shoulder dystocia training, enabling medical professionals to practice emergency procedures in an interactive virtual environment. {{< extlink href="https://repositorium.meduniwien.ac.at/obvumwoa/content/titleinfo/11371835/full.pdf" >}}The underlying study{{< /extlink >}} showed that VR training is a feasible and effective method, improving performance and reducing response times through repetition.

Additionally, I worked on:

- A digital twin of the F&P Neopuff T-Piece in collaboration with Fisher & Paykel Healthcare, requiring high accuracy and functional realism.
- A contamination visualization system with Schülke & Mayr, making hygiene-related interactions and cross-contamination visible during training scenarios.

Overall, my role focused on delivering high-quality, optimized 3D assets that supported realistic, interactive, and performant VR training experiences.
